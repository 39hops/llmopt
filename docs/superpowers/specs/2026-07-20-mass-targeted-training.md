# Mass-targeted training — stop paying for what the model knows

Provenance: Artin 2026-07-20, 2 AM ("epochs are brutal... is there
an efficient way to funnel data into the correct neurons based off
our equation?"). The equation's answer: the calculator knows each
family's exposure, the committee table knows each family's
crystallization (mass), and texture freezes early — so uniform
epochs re-feed mastered circuits every pass. The epoch killer is
not faster arithmetic; it is a smaller, aimed corpus.

## Rung A — mass-targeted diet (cheap; next birth)

Per family/level, classify by the mass spectrum:
- HEAVY (mastered: committee selectivity high, gate saturated,
  e.g. L1-L3, power family): maintenance ration only (~10-20% of
  current rows — enough to prevent drift, measured not guessed).
- LIGHT (underfed: low selectivity, gate gaps, fresh territory):
  full feed + the new farmed mass.

Predicted effects: epoch wall drops roughly with corpus size
(uniform gen-6 = 178k rows; targeted plausibly ~90-110k), and the
650k-row perfection price falls toward ~half (it assumed uniform
feeding). A/B: mass-targeted vs uniform at EQUAL WALL-CLOCK
(not equal rows), same honest gate + probes. Risk pre-registered:
catastrophic drift on rationed families — the maintenance ration
is the knob, and the gate's saturated levels are the alarm.

## Rung B — committee-gated plasticity (one optimizer hook)

Precision-follows-rarity, in the optimizer: per-neuron LR scaled
by measured mass — heavy/mastered circuits near-frozen, light
circuits hot. The committee probe (minutes, per birth) supplies
the mask; the biography says roles stay fluid, so the mask is
refreshed per epoch, not trusted across the run. This is the
banked importance-weighted-training riff made concrete by the
mass spectrum. A/B rides the same gate as Rung A; run AFTER A
so the two effects never confound.
